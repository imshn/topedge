# What is Performance Marketing and How Does It Boost ROI?

**Published:** July 2026 | **Reading Time:** 7 min | **By:** Top Edge MediaWorks

## Performance Marketing: Defined

Marketing where you only pay for results. No views. No impressions. **You pay only when someone clicks, leads, or buys.**

---

## How It Works

1. Set KPI (Key Performance Indicator): ₹500 per lead, ₹10,000 per sale
2. Run campaigns (Google Ads, Meta Ads, affiliate marketing)
3. Only pay when goal achieved
4. Track ROI constantly

---

## Example: E-commerce Store

**Traditional Marketing:** Pay ₹50,000 in ads. Hope for sales.

**Performance Marketing:** Pay ₹500 per sale. Only pay for actual sales.

- If 100 people click → 10 buy → You pay: ₹5,000 (10 × ₹500)
- Revenue: ₹1,00,000 (10 sales)
- Profit: ₹95,000
- ROI: 1900%

---

## Key Metrics

**CPC (Cost Per Click):** ₹50-300
**CPL (Cost Per Lead):** ₹500-2,000
**CPA (Cost Per Acquisition):** ₹2,000-10,000
**ROAS (Return on Ad Spend):** 3:1 to 5:1 = goal

---

## Types of Performance Marketing

- Google Ads (search)
- Meta Ads (social)
- Affiliate marketing
- Email marketing (if tracked)
- Influencer marketing (pay per result)

---

## Case Study: B2B SaaS

**Company:** Project management software
**Goal:** Get paying customers
**Traditional: ₹5L/month budget → uncertain results**
**Performance Marketing: Pay only per signup**

- Cost per trial signup: ₹2,000
- Cost per paid customer: ₹15,000
- Monthly paid customers: 15-20
- Monthly revenue: ₹30,00,000

**Result:** Predictable, scalable growth. Zero wasted budget.

| Traditional | Performance |

## FAQ

**Q: Isn't performance marketing expensive?**
A: No. You only pay for results. Much cheaper long-term.

**Q: How do I measure performance?**
A: Google Analytics + tracking pixels + CRM integration.

**Q: What CPA should I target?**
A: If customer lifetime value = ₹50,000, CPA should be ₹5,000-10,000 max.
